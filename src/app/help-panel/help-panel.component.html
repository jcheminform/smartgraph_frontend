Control Panel
Descriptions and Definitions
Start nodes:

Please enter a list of NS-InChi keys (first block of standard InChi key) of compounds and/or UniProt identifiers of targets. The list can be separated by spaces, commas and line separator.


End nodes (optional):

Please enter a list of UniProt IDs of destination targets. The list can be separated by spaces, commas and line separator. Providing end node(s) is optional.


Activity (-log(M)):

Bioactivity filter controls which drug-target interactions (DTIs) to consider in path discovery. DTIs with aggregated bioactivity value greater than or equal to this value will be considered, other DTIs will be filtered out. This feature provides the option to include or exclude weak DTIs in network perturbation analysis. The unit of the bioactivity filter is -log(M), which is often denoted by pChEMBL.


Max Distance:

Parameter to control the “depth” of network perturbation analysis, i.e. path discovery process. While high values will allow for finding relatively long paths between the ‘Start Nodes’ (small molecule(s) and/or target(s)), and the ‘Destination Nodes’, it should be considered that long-distance effects might be less and less deterministic.


PPI Confidence Level:

Each protein-protein interaction (PPI) is attributed by a confidence value defined by the authors of the SIGNOR 2.0 database. PPIs of greater than or equal to this parameter will be only considered in the path discovery process.


Link View Column Headers
Descriptions and Definitions
Source:

The protein target or compound that regulates the protein target in the “Target” column.


Link Type:

Provides information on the type of relationship between a given pair of nodes. Please note, that the relationships (edges) are directed.


Target:

The protein target regulated by the protein target or compound in the “Source” column.


Details:

A protein-protein interaction between protein targets A and B, i.e. PPI(A, B), is annotated by the mechanism of the regulation. A given PPI(A,B) might be associated by multiple mechanisms which are displayed in individual rows along with the respective confidence score and references. Source: SIGNOR 2.0 database.


Reference:

PubMed identifier (PMID) of the reference associated with a given protein-protein interaction (PPI). Source: SIGNOR 2.0 database.


Score:

Drug-target interactions (DTIs), protein-protein interactions (PPI) and a pattern-compound relationship are attributed by a bioactivity, confidence value, and pattern overlap ratio, respectively. The appropriate attribute is shown in this column depending the relationship type at hand. Bioactivity is expressed in units of micromolar (denoted: uM)and were collected from the ChEMBL database and aggregated according to the workflow presented in the SmartGraph paper. A confidence value of a PPI (range: 0-1, 1 being highest confidence) was defined by the authors of the SIGNOR 2.0 database as the overlap ratio between a pattern and a compound


Max. Confidence:

Between two targets multiple types of interactions may have been identified by the authors of the SIGNOR 2.0 database. Interactions are associated with a confidence score (0-1, 1 indicating highest confidence). The ‘Max. Confidence’ value reflects the maximal observed confidence value among any interaction defined between protein targets A and B, where A is the regulator and B is regulated by A.
